Introduction

How many of us face problems when your Apple Watch asks you to stand up when you are already standing? Well, that happens to me! ... Or even when you're on a motored two-wheeler (motorbike, etc.), and the Watch or the phone detects that you are cycling. That is a Machine Learning algorithm wrongly detecting similar actions, along with change in location.

This Machine Learning projects looks at all preliminary actions humans would perform - sitting, standing, laying, walking, walking downstairs, and walking upstairs. With multiple models used, their accuracy is compared, and wrongly recognised actions are compared to assess why they might be wrongly recognised. Is there something wrong with the data, or is that the actions are very similar which makes it challenging for models to distinguish?
